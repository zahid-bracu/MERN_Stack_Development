GRE Corner

What is GRE?

The Graduate Record Examination , or GRE, is an important step in the graduate school or business school application process.

Why GRE?

Most people take the exam in order to get into grad school or to be considered for merit-based fellowships for grad school. Because thousands of grad programs and business schools around the world accept GRE scores, it’s likely you’ll need to take the GRE if you are ever planning to enroll in a master’s or doctoral program.

The purpose of the GRE is to measure applicants’ preparedness for grad school and their potential for academic success .Grad schools use GRE scores to compare applicants with one another.

Who Conducts GRE Exam?

The GRE exam is designed and administered by the Educational Testing Service or ETS .They conduct the examination globally.
